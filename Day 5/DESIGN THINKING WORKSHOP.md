***DESIGN IS FUN*** : Create curiosity

**What is Design Thinking**
Design Thinking is a human - centered and iterative approach to problem-solving and innovation. It emphasizes understanding the needs, challenges, and desires of end-users and stakeholders to create innovative and meaningful solutions. Co-creating with your users and aim is what the user will achieve. It is non-linear meaning it is iterative.

**Elements of Design Thinking**
- Empathize
- Define
- Ideate
- Protoype
- Test

**Design Challenge**
1. Design Challenge 1 : As Ecobank continues to grow its ecosystem and revenue, how might we design an onboarding experience that is not just secure but truly accessible for different types of users while still making it easy for them to quickly see value and stay engaged.
2. Design Challenge 2: How might we better support small business and entrepreneurs within Ecobank's ecosystem by designing solutions that simplify their financial operations and help them grow sustainably.

**Empathy**
Understand their Pains. Empathy is our ability to see the world through other people's eyes, to see what they see, feel what they feel, and experience things as they do. As we so often like to say, walk in another person's shoes.
In designing thinking, Empathy is a deep understanding of the problems and realities of the people you're designing for - IDEO
Key factor of Empathy - Listening, body language.
Ask why 5 time (A saying in designing thinking)

**Why Empathize**
1. To create desirable products;
2. Helps uncover intangible needs and feelings, that indicate what should ideally change in the product, system, or environment we're focusing on.
3. Empathic research reveals the deeper needs and root causes, which, if addressed correctly, may profoundly change the project we're investigating.
4. People ignore design that ignores people - Frank Chimero

**Ideation Techniques**
- Logical: Loves the common way (follow/make patterns), Evaluates immediately dislikes uncertainties, Wants to stop after a solution, Based on knowledge and experience.
- Creative: Hates routine, Looking for originality, Embraces uncertainties, One solution leads to another option, Directed to the future. 

**The Six Thinking Hats**
The hats or thinking modes, when used proactively separate ego from performance, so that everyone works toward a goal rather than in defense of a position.

- The White Hat (Facts)
- The Yellow Hat  (Benefits)
- The Black Hat (Caution)
- The Red Hat (Feelings)
- The Green Hat (Creativity)
- The Blue Hat (Process)

**Protyping**
A protype is a simple experimental model of a proposed solution used to test or validate ideas, design assumptions and other aspects of its conceptualization quickly and cheaply, so that the designer/s involved can make appropriate refinements or possible changes in direction.
