**What is Product Management**
A strategic discipline guiding a product from concept market success by understanding customers and delivering business. A product manager is the CEO of a product.

**Strategic Role**
Define vision and outcomes, align cross-functional teams, prioritize work that matters,  and ensure the product solves real customer needs.

**Key Intersections:**
1. Customer Empathy - Focuses on understanding real user problems and needs to ensure solutions are relevant and impactful.
2. Business Objectives - Aligns with organizational goals to guarantee the viability and sustainability of the solution.
3. Technology Realities - Considers technical constraints and possibilities to build what is feasible.
4. Design Excellence - Prioritize usability and delight, crafting experiences that are intuitive and engaging.

**The Product Manager as Influencer and Leader**
1. Influence over Authority
2. Decision-Making Clarity
3. Storytelling Power
4. Data-Driven Leadership

**Managing Stakeholders as a PM**
1. Identify your stakeholders - Executives; Marketing, Sales, Ops; Engineering and Design; Finance and Compliance; Customers; Regulators.
2. Understand needs and motivations - Map goals, risks, and decision power to craft clear, value-aligned narratives. 
	***Ask*** - 
	- What does success look like to them? - 
	- What do they fear? - 
	- How do they prefer communication?
3. Communicate proactively - PMs never wait until there is a problem. They:
	- Share roadmaps
	- Give status updates
	- Present data-driven decisions
	- Provide trade-offs clearly
	Golden rule: "No surprises" 
4. Manage conflicts with clarity - Conflict are inevitable, prioritization means saying "no" often. You handle this by:
	- Linking decisions to OKRs
	- Using a prioritization framework (RICE, MoSCoW, Kano)
	- Explaining impact versus effort
	- Showing customer feedback and data

**Essential Product Terminology**
1. Strategy and Planning - Vision, Roadmap, North Star, Metric, OKRs,  and KPIs are essential for setting direction and measuring progress in product management.
2. Discovery - Personas, problem statement, journey maps, user interviews, surveys, and JBTD help uncover user needs and validate solutions.
3. Vision and Roadmap - Vision defines the long-term goal; Roadmap outlines the steps to get .
4. User Research Tools - Personas, Interviews

**Analytics and Key Metrics**
1. Activation - Measures quickly how users start using the product.
2. Engagement - Tracks user activity, frequency
3. Retention - Shows the percentage of users who continue using the product over time.
4. Churn - Rate at which users stop using the product.
